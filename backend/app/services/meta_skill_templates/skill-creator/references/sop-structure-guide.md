# MR Training SOP Structure Guide

## Overview

A pharmaceutical Medical Representative (MR) training SOP defines the standard
conversation flow for face-to-face (F2F) interactions with Healthcare
Professionals (HCPs). A well-structured SOP ensures consistent, professional,
and effective communication.

## Required Stages (5 Stages)

Every MR training SOP MUST include these 5 stages in order:

### Stage 1: Opening (建立关系)
**Purpose**: Establish rapport, set the agenda, create a professional first impression.

**Quality criteria**:
- Greeting is appropriate for the setting (clinic, hospital, conference)
- MR introduces themselves and their company clearly
- Agenda is stated with a time estimate (e.g., "I'd like to share 3 key findings from a recent study — about 5 minutes")
- Transition to needs assessment is natural

**Common errors**:
- Jumping directly to product information without rapport
- No time frame given — HCP feels trapped
- Generic opening that doesn't adapt to setting

**Suggested duration**: 1-2 minutes

### Stage 2: Needs Assessment (需求评估)
**Purpose**: Discover the HCP's current prescribing patterns, unmet needs, and knowledge gaps.

**Quality criteria**:
- At least 2-3 open-ended discovery questions
- Questions are relevant to the product's indication
- MR listens actively and adapts based on responses
- Transitions smoothly to product discussion based on identified needs

**Common errors**:
- Asking only closed questions (yes/no)
- Not connecting HCP responses to the product discussion
- Skipping this stage entirely

**Suggested duration**: 2-3 minutes

### Stage 3: Product Discussion (产品讨论)
**Purpose**: Present key product messages with supporting clinical evidence, tailored to the HCP's identified needs.

**Quality criteria**:
- 3-5 key messages prioritized by relevance to HCP's needs
- Each key message is supported by specific clinical data (trial name, endpoints, p-values)
- Competitive differentiation is included when appropriate
- Visuals/aids are referenced (e.g., "As you can see in this graph from the ALPINE trial...")
- Language is scientific but accessible

**Common errors**:
- Dumping all product information without tailoring
- No clinical evidence to support claims
- Missing competitive context when HCP asks
- Using marketing language instead of clinical language

**Suggested duration**: 5-8 minutes

### Stage 4: Objection Handling (异议处理)
**Purpose**: Address HCP concerns and objections with evidence-based responses.

**Quality criteria**:
- At least 2-4 common objections anticipated
- Each objection has a structured response: acknowledge → reframe → provide evidence → check understanding
- Responses cite specific clinical data when possible
- MR remains empathetic and non-confrontational
- Objections cover: efficacy, safety, cost, switching concerns, dosing

**Common errors**:
- Dismissing objections or being defensive
- Responding without evidence
- Not acknowledging the validity of the concern
- Only preparing for one type of objection

**Suggested duration**: 3-5 minutes

### Stage 5: Closing (收尾总结)
**Purpose**: Summarize key points, agree on next steps, and secure a follow-up commitment.

**Quality criteria**:
- Concise summary of 2-3 key takeaways
- Specific next-step request (not vague "I'll follow up")
- Leave-behind materials referenced
- Thank the HCP for their time
- Follow-up timeline agreed

**Common errors**:
- Ending abruptly without summary
- No clear call-to-action or next step
- Missing opportunity to provide additional resources

**Suggested duration**: 1-2 minutes

## Quality Checklist

For a complete SOP, verify:
- [ ] All 5 stages present in correct order
- [ ] Each stage has key_points, objections, and assessment_criteria
- [ ] Total suggested duration is 12-20 minutes (realistic F2F call)
- [ ] At least 3 key messages in Product Discussion
- [ ] At least 2 objection scenarios in Objection Handling
- [ ] Clinical trial names and data points are specific (not generic)
- [ ] Knowledge points are factual and verifiable
- [ ] Assessment criteria are observable and measurable
