# Common HCP Objections — Zanubrutinib

## Objection 1: "I'm already comfortable with ibrutinib"
**Context**: Many hematologists have years of experience with ibrutinib and are
reluctant to switch to a newer agent.

**Key data points to address**:
- ALPINE trial showed statistically superior ORR (78.3% vs 62.5%, p=0.0006)
- Significantly lower atrial fibrillation rate (2.5% vs 10.1%)
- Lower major hemorrhage rate
- Superior PFS demonstrated

## Objection 2: "What about long-term safety data?"
**Context**: Physicians want long-term follow-up data before switching from established
therapies.

**Key data points**:
- Median follow-up in ALPINE: 29.6 months
- Consistent safety profile across multiple trials (ALPINE, ASPEN, SEQUOIA)
- Lower cardiovascular toxicity compared to ibrutinib based on BTK selectivity

## Objection 3: "My patients are doing well on their current regimen"
**Context**: For patients already stable on ibrutinib, switching may seem unnecessary.

**Key data points**:
- Address proactively for NEW patients, not necessarily switching stable patients
- For new treatment decisions, BRUKINSA offers better risk-benefit profile
- NCCN guidelines list zanubrutinib as preferred option for CLL/SLL

## Objection 4: "Is the twice-daily dosing inconvenient?"
**Context**: Ibrutinib is once daily; zanubrutinib has a BID option.

**Key data points**:
- 320 mg QD (once daily) option is also available
- Flexibility in dosing schedule based on patient preference
- Clinical efficacy demonstrated with both BID and QD dosing
