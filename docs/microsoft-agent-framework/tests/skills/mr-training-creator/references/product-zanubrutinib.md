# Zanubrutinib (BRUKINSA) - Product Reference

## Overview
Zanubrutinib (brand name BRUKINSA) is a next-generation, highly selective Bruton's
tyrosine kinase (BTK) inhibitor developed by BeiGene for the treatment of various
B-cell malignancies.

## Indications
- Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL) / Small Lymphocytic Lymphoma (SLL)
- Waldenström's Macroglobulinemia (WM)
- Mantle Cell Lymphoma (MCL) in patients who have received at least one prior therapy
- Marginal Zone Lymphoma (MZL) who have received at least one anti-CD20-based regimen

## Mechanism of Action
Zanubrutinib inhibits BTK by forming a covalent bond with a cysteine residue in the
BTK active site. It is designed to be more selective for BTK than first-generation
inhibitors (ibrutinib), resulting in fewer off-target effects.

## Key Clinical Data

### ALPINE Trial (CLL/SLL)
- **Design**: Phase 3, randomized, head-to-head vs ibrutinib in relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL
- **Primary endpoint**: Overall Response Rate (ORR)
- **Results**:
  - ORR: zanubrutinib 78.3% vs ibrutinib 62.5% (p=0.0006)
  - Superior PFS with zanubrutinib
  - Lower rate of atrial fibrillation: 2.5% vs 10.1%
  - Lower rate of major hemorrhage

### ASPEN Trial (WM)
- **Design**: Phase 3, randomized vs ibrutinib in WM patients with MYD88 mutation
- **Results**:
  - VGPR rate: zanubrutinib 28.4% vs ibrutinib 19.2%
  - Lower cardiac adverse events

## Dosage
- 160 mg twice daily OR 320 mg once daily (oral capsules)
- Take with or without food

## Common Adverse Events
- Neutropenia (most common Grade 3+)
- Upper respiratory tract infection
- Bruising
- Diarrhea
